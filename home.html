Parts of the Turn

>   Begin

  1. Untap
  2. Draw

∨ Beginning Phase

Untap Step

  1. Untap all permanents
  • No one can cast spells or activate abilities during this step

Upkeep Step

  1. Resolve any abilities that trigger at "the beginning of the upkeep"
  2. Upkeep costs are paid

Draw Step

  1. Active player draws a card.
  2. Resolve any abilities that trigger at "the beginning of the draw step"

∨ Beginning Phase

Untap Step

  1. Phasing in and out (simultaneously)
  2. Untap all permanents (simultaneously)
  • state-based effects and triggered abilities are delayed to the upkeep

Upkeep Step

  1. Any abilities that trigger at the beginning of the upkeep go on the stack
  2. The active player gains priority
  3. *upkeep costs are paid(when?)

Draw Step

  1. Active player draws a card.
  2. Any abilities that trigger at the beginning of the draw step go on the stack
  3. The active player gains priority

>   Main Phase

  • Play a land (only one land per turn)
  • Cast creatures and other spells

∨ First Main Phase

In any order:

  • Once per turn, the active player may play a land from his or her hand
  • Cast creatures and other spells

∨ Precombat Main Phase

  1. Abilities that trigger at the beginning of the main phase or the precombat main phase go on the stack
  2. Active player gains priority
    • Once per turn, the active player may play a land from their hand while the stack is empty (This is a special action that doesn't use the stack)
    • Non-instants can be played during this phase, by the active player, when the stack is empty

>   Combat

  1. Declare Attackers
  2. Opponents declare blockers
  3. Combat damage is dealt

∨ Combat Phase

Beginning of Combat Step

  1. Choose all players that you wish to attack

Declare Attackers Step

  1. Assign attacking creatures
    • Must be untapped
    • Must have haste or have been controlled by the active player continuously since the turn began
  2. Assign which creatures are attacking which players
  3. Tap creatures that are attacking
  4. Pay for optional attacking costs, if desired
  5. Abilities trigger for attacking creatures being declared

Declare Blockers Step

  1. The defending player chooses which creatures they control to block
    • Must be untapped
    • The defending player chooses which creatures will block which attacking creatures
    • More than one creature may block a single attacking creature
    1. Any attacking creature with one or more creatures declares as blocking for it becomes a blocked creature; any without creatures blocking them become unblocked creatures
    2. Abilities trigger for blockers being declared
  2. For each attacking creature that's become blocked, announce that creature's damage assignment order
  3. For each blocking creature, the defending player announces that creatures's damage assignment order

Combat Damage

  1. Announce how each attacking creature assigns its combat damage
  2. The defending player announces how each blocking creature assigns its combat damage
  3. Each attacking creature and blocking creature assigns combat damage equal to its power
  4. All combat damage that's been assigned is dealt simultaneously
  • If a creature has first strike or double strike:
    1. As the combat damage step begins, the only creatures that assign combat damage in that step are those with first strike or double strike
    2. Instead of proceeding to the end of combat step, the phase gets a second combat damage step
    3. The only creatures that assign combat damage in that step are the remaining attackers and blockers that had neither first strike nor double strike as the first combat damage step began, as well as the remaining attackers and blockers that currently have double strike
    4. After that step, the phase proceeds to the end of combat step

End of Combat Step

  1. Abilities that trigger "at the end of combat" trigger
  2. The active player gets priority
  3. Creatures and planeswalkers are removed from combat and effects that last "until end of combat" expire

∨ Combat Phase

Beginning of Combat Step

  1. Choose all players that you wish to attack
  2. Active player gains priority

Declare Attackers Step

  1. The active playerssign attacking creatures
    • Must be untapped
    • Must have haste or have been controlled by the active player continuously since the turn began
  2. Active player assigns which creatures are attacking which players
  3. If applicable, the active player anounces Banding / "bands with other" which creatures are banded
  4. Active player taps all creatures that are attacking
  5. The active player can choose to pay for optional attacking costs
  6. The active player may activate mana sources and once they have enough for all costs, pays them in any order in full
  7. Each chosen creature still controlled by the active player is an attacting creature
  8. Abilities trigger for attacking creatures being declared
  9. Active player gains priority

Declare Blockers Step

  1. The defending player chooses which creatures they control to block
    • The chosen creatures must be untapped
    • The defending player chooses which creatures will block which attacking creatures
    • More than one creature may block a single attacking creature, unless stated otherwise
    • Defending creatures must obey any and all restrictions and requirements
    1. If any of the chosen creatures require paying costs to block, the defending player determines the total cost to block
    2. If any of the cost require mana, the defending player then has a chanse to activate mana abilities
    3. One the player has enough mana in their mana pool, they pay all costs in any order in full
    4. Each chosen creature still controlled by the defending player becomes a blocking creature
      • A blocking creature remains as such until is is removed from combat or the combat phase ends
    5. Any attacking creature with one or more creatures declares as blocking for it becomes a blocked creature; any without creatures blocking them become unblocked creatures
      • This remains unchanged until the creature is removed form combat, an effect says that it is blocked or unblocked, or the combat phase ends, which ever comes first
    6. Abilities trigger for blockers being declared
    7. the active player gets priority
      • Any abilities that triggered on blockers being declared or that trigger during the process are put onto the stack before the active player gets priority
  2. For each attacking creature that's become blocked, the active player announces that creature's damage assignment order
  3. For each blocking creature, the defending player announces that creatures's damage assignment order

Combat Damage

  1. The active player announces how each attacking creature assigns its combat damage
  2. The defending player announces how each blocking creature assigns its combat damage
  3. Each attacking creature and blocking creature assigns combat damage equal to its power
    • An unblocked creature assigns its combat damage to the player or planeswalker it's attacking
    • A blocked creature assigns its combat damage to the creatures blocking it according to the damage assignment order announced for it
      • This may allow the blocked creature to divide its combat damage. However, it can’t assign combat damage to a creature that’s blocking it unless, when combat damage assignments are complete, each creature that precedes that blocking creature in its order is assigned lethal damage. When checking for assigned lethal damage, take into account damage already marked on the creature and damage from other creatures that’s being assigned during the same combat damage step, but not any abilities or effects that might change the amount of damage that’s actually dealt. An amount of damage that’s greater than a creature’s lethal damage may be assigned to it.
    • A blocking creature assigns combat damage to the creatures it's blocking according to the damage assignment order announced for it
      • This may allow the blocking creature to divide its combat damage. However, it can’t assign combat damage to a creature that it’s blocking unless, when combat damage assignments are complete, each creature that precedes that blocked creature is assigned lethal damage. When checking for assigned lethal damage, take into account damage already marked on the creature and damage from other creatures that’s being assigned during the same combat damage step, but not any abilities or effects that might change the amount of damage that’s actually dealt. An amount of damage that’s greater than a creature’s lethal damage may be assigned to it.
  4. All combat damage that's been assigned is dealt simultaneously
  5. The active player gets priority
    • Any abilities that triggered are put onto the stack first
  • If a creature has first strike or double strike:
    1. As the combat damage step begins, the only creatures that assign combat damage in that step are those with first strike or double strike
    2. Instead of proceeding to the end of combat step, the phase gets a second combat damage step
    3. The only creatures that assign combat damage in that step are the remaining attackers and blockers that had neither first strike nor double strike as the first combat damage step began, as well as the remaining attackers and blockers that currently have double strike
    4. After that step, the phase proceeds to the end of combat step

End of Combat Step

  1. Abilities that trigger "at the end of combat" trigger
  2. The active player gets priority
  3. Creatures and planeswalkers are removed from combat and effects that last "until end of combat" expire

>   End

  1. Discard down to seven cards in hand
  2. Creatures Heal

∨ End Phase

End Step

  1. Resolve any abilities that trigger "at end of turn", "at the beginning of the end step", or "at the beginning of the next end step"

Cleanup Step

  1. Discard down to your maximum hand size
  2. All damage is removed and all "until end of turn" and "this turn" effects end

∨ Ending Phase

End Step

  1. Any abilities that trigger "at end of turn", "at the beginning of the end step", or "at the beginning of the next end step" go on the stack
  2. The active player gets priority

Cleanup Step

  1. The active player discards down to their maximum hand size
  2. All damage marked on permanents is removed and all "until end of turn" and "this turn" effects end
  3. If any triggered abilities are put on the stack, then the active player gets priority
    • Once the stack is empty, another cleanup step begins
